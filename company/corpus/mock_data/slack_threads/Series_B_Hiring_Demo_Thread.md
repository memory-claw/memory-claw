**🔎 Memory** [9:20 AM]

> **This is the same reasoning as the H1 2023 hiring decision.**
>
> In January 2023, the team made the decision to hire aggressively ahead of a Series A "expected to close in Q2." Between February and May, 5 people were hired. The Series A did not close on timeline. By July, runway was 4.2 months at the new burn rate.
>
> In September 2023, 2 of those engineers were let go — 4–6 months after they'd joined, turned down other offers, and in some cases relocated.
>
> The hiring policy (October 2023) requires that hiring is triggered by received funding or revenue that supports the hire at 18 months runway — not by an expected term sheet. "Sequoia expects to move to term sheet in 6 weeks" is not received funding. Term sheets can take longer than expected or not close at all.
>
> The alternative worth considering: prepare the job descriptions and interviewing pipeline now, move fast once funds are received. Time-to-hire from a standing start is usually 6–8 weeks — roughly the same as the expected term sheet timeline.
>
