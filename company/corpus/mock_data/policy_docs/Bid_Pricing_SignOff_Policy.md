# PROCESS POLICY
## Bid Pricing & Delivery Sign-Off Requirements

**Author:** James Liu, Delivery Lead  
**Date:** 2023-08-01  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** MANDATORY — All bids over £250,000  
**Risk Level:** High  
**Department:** Sales / Delivery / Finance  
**Outcome:** Zero margin-negative bids since adoption  

---

### Background

In H1 2023, we delivered the FinCo Analytics Platform on time and at a total loss of £640,000. The contract was priced at £1.1M. Delivery cost £1.74M. The pricing did not include delivery complexity estimates, third-party licensing, or risk contingency. There was no mechanism to renegotiate scope once discovered.

This policy prevents recurrence.

---

### Mandatory Bid Approval Gates

**For all bids over £250,000:**

#### Gate 1 — Delivery Complexity Estimate
- James Liu or a designated senior delivery lead must review scope and provide a written complexity estimate before pricing is finalised
- Estimate must include: team size, timeline, risk factors, assumptions

#### Gate 2 — Third-Party Licensing Review
- All required third-party software, tooling, or API costs must be itemised
- "TBC" is not acceptable in a bid at this stage
- FinCo required £180,000 in additional licensing that was not in the price

#### Gate 3 — Risk Contingency (Minimum 15%)
- All bids must include a minimum 15% risk contingency on the delivery estimate
- Contingency must be a visible line item, not absorbed into day rates

#### Gate 4 — Scope Change Clause
- Rachel Moore's standard scope change clause must be included in all contracts
- Client must agree in writing to a formal change control process before contract execution
- Changes to scope without a signed change request are not funded

#### Gate 5 — CFO Approval (Bids over £500,000)
- CFO must review margin model before submission
- Target minimum margin: 22%
- Bids below 15% margin require CFO written exception

---

### Why This Exists

*"We won the deal and lost the company money."* — James Liu, July 2023

The FinCo engagement cost us £640,000. It happened because one person (Sales) priced a contract without input from Delivery, Finance, or Compliance. This policy ensures that never happens again.

If a bid is in progress and you hear someone say "we'll figure out the costs once we've won it" — that is the FinCo conversation. Stop it.
