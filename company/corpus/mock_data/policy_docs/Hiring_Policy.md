# COMPANY POLICY
## Engineering Hiring — Revenue Trigger Requirements

**Author:** Priya Patel  
**Date:** 2023-10-01  
**Status:** MANDATORY — All engineering headcount decisions  
**Related:** Hiring retrospective October 2023 — 2 layoffs after over-hiring in H1 2023  

---

### Background

In H1 2023, we hired 4 engineers and 1 designer ahead of a Series A that did not close on the expected timeline. Monthly burn increased by $62,000. In September 2023, we laid off 2 engineers. The layoffs affected people who had joined in good faith months earlier.

This policy exists so we never make that trade-off again.

---

### The Rule

**We hire when we have the revenue or funding to support it. Not when we expect to.**

A term sheet is not funding. A verbal from an investor is not funding. An enterprise deal in final negotiations is not ARR.

---

### Headcount Triggers

A new engineering hire can be approved when one of the following is true:

#### Trigger A — Revenue Supported
Current ARR, divided by 12, must cover the new hire's fully-loaded monthly cost **and** maintain minimum 18 months of runway at new burn rate.

Example:  
ARR: $800K → monthly revenue: $67K  
New hire fully-loaded cost: $12K/month  
New burn rate must still give 18+ months runway  

If the math doesn't work: the hire waits until it does.

#### Trigger B — Funding Closed
A funding round is received (funds in bank account). Not term sheet signed. Not "expected to close next month." Funds received.

Hiring can begin within 30 days of funds received.

#### Trigger C — Board Exception
Priya and board explicitly approve a hire outside triggers A and B, with full documentation of: the runway impact, the risk if projected revenue/funding doesn't materialise, and a contingency plan.

Board exceptions require unanimous board approval.

---

### What "Fully-Loaded Cost" Means

Salary + employer taxes + benefits + equipment + SaaS tooling per seat.  
Rule of thumb: 1.3x–1.4x base salary.

Do not model on base salary alone. This is how burn surprises happen.

---

### Before Opening Any Role

Before posting a job description or engaging a recruiter, Priya must document:

1. Which trigger applies
2. The runway impact (before and after the hire, at current burn)
3. Why the hire is necessary now vs. in 3–6 months

This takes 30 minutes. The September 2023 layoffs took 6 months of productivity loss, significant team trust damage, and real human cost to two employees.

---

### The Human Reminder

When we hire someone, they make decisions based on our offer: they sign leases, turn down other jobs, sometimes relocate. If we let them go 6 months later because we over-hired, those consequences fall on them, not on us.

Hire when it's sustainable. Not when it's exciting.
